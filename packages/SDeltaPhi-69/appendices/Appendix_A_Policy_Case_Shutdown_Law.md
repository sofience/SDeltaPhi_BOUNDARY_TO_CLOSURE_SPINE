# Appendix A — Policy Case: Shutdown Law

## Purpose of this appendix

This case is an illustrative application, not an exhaustive policy history. It is used to show the distinction between policy repeal and erasure of transition history.

## SΔϕ-69 diagnosis

A legal regime may be repealed, but the world that passed through that regime is not identical to a world in which the regime never existed.

In the Korean shutdown-law case, the relevant structure is:

```text
policy enactment → compliance architecture → cultural and industrial adaptation → policy repeal → residual costs
```

The repeal of a law may restore a surface permission. It does not erase the compliance systems, institutional habits, public stigma, user experiences, or platform adaptations generated during the period of enforcement.

## Core formulation

> The shutdown law may be abolished. But abolition is not a return to a world in which the shutdown law never existed.

Korean formulation:

> 셧다운제는 폐지될 수 있다. 그러나 셧다운제가 없었던 세계로 돌아간 것은 아니다.

## TCC layer

SΔϕ-56 TCC may be used to estimate:

- enactment cost,
- compliance cost,
- enforcement cost,
- repeal and transition cost,
- residual cultural and institutional cost.

## Caution

This appendix does not evaluate every empirical effect of the shutdown law. It only marks the structural point: repeal may overwrite the legal surface, but it does not erase transition history.

# Appendix B — Historical Case: Colonial Rule and Liberation

## Purpose of this appendix

This case is a macro-historical illustration. It is not a total causal explanation of modern Korean history. It is included only to distinguish the termination of a regime from the erasure of the transition history created by that regime.

## SΔϕ-69 diagnosis

The end of colonial rule may terminate the surface regime of occupation or annexation. It does not restore the pre-colonial state as though the intervening period never occurred.

For the Korean case, the structural formulation is:

```text
colonial rule → institutional and cultural transformation → imperial collapse → liberation → occupation / division / war / continuing division costs
```

The point is not to claim that every later consequence has one cause. The point is more limited: liberation ended colonial rule, but it did not erase the transition history generated by colonial rule and imperial collapse.

## Core formulation

> Liberation may end colonial rule, but it does not return a society to a world in which colonial rule never occurred.

Korean formulation:

> 해방은 식민 지배의 종료였지만, 식민 지배가 없었던 세계로의 복귀는 아니었다.

## SΔϕ-69 reading

```text
surface change: colonial rule terminated
transition history: the society passed through colonial administration, institutional deformation, cultural injury, geopolitical reordering, and post-imperial transition
residual irreversibility: memory, law, institutions, borders, conflict structures, diplomatic disputes, cultural loss, division costs
```

## Caution

This appendix must not be used as a single-cause explanation of division, war, or contemporary politics. It is a structural example of apparent reversal: the surface regime ended, but the passage through that regime remains part of the historical state.

# SΔϕ-69 Failure Modes

## 1. Over-totalization

Error: claiming that no restoration can meaningfully matter.

Correction: SΔϕ-69 distinguishes restoration from non-occurrence. It does not deny practical repair.

## 2. Causal overreach

Error: collapsing complex historical outcomes into a single cause.

Correction: use SΔϕ-69 only to mark transition-history residue, not to provide total causal explanation.

## 3. Residue inflation

Error: claiming residue exists without evidence or exaggerating its magnitude.

Correction: mark unknowns and use TCC or evidence-based proxies where available.

## 4. Surface dismissal

Error: ignoring real benefits of restoration, repeal, repair, or rollback.

Correction: surface restoration may reduce harm while still preserving transition history.

## 5. Moralization drift

Error: converting structural diagnosis into personal accusation.

Correction: diagnose path structure, not character, unless evidence requires agency analysis.

## 6. Case capture

Error: allowing a politically charged appendix case to dominate the abstract principle.

Correction: keep main diagnosis abstract; use cases as illustrations, not proof by emotion.

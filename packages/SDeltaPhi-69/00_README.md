# SΔϕ-69 — Reversibility as Overwrite: Apparent Restoration, Transition History, and Residual Irreversibility

**Korean title:** SΔϕ-69 — 가역성의 덮어쓰기 원리: 표면 복원, 전이 이력, 잔여 비가역성  
**Version:** v1.0  
**Provisional DOI:** `10.5281/zenodo.20375357`  
**Package type:** AI-READABLE working-paper package

## One-sentence thesis

**Reversibility is overwrite, not true return.** A system may be reconstructed to resemble a prior surface state, but the transition history and residual cost of having changed are not erased.

## Korean thesis

**가역은 되돌림이 아니라 덮어쓰기다.** 이전 상태와 유사한 표면은 재구성될 수 있으나, 그 상태가 변화했다는 사실과 그 변화가 남긴 비용은 폐기되지 않는다.

## What this package is for

This package defines SΔϕ-69 as a diagnostic principle for cases involving rollback, repeal, restoration, memory reset, policy reversal, legal abolition, relationship repair, historical liberation, institutional reform, data recovery, and AI checkpoint rollback.

The module distinguishes:

1. surface equivalence,
2. transition history,
3. overwrite cost,
4. residual irreversibility,
5. TCC-based restoration cost.

## What this package is not for

SΔϕ-69 does **not** claim that restoration is impossible. It claims that restoration is not identical to non-occurrence. It must not be used to collapse complex history into a single cause, to inflate residue beyond evidence, or to convert structural diagnosis into moral accusation.

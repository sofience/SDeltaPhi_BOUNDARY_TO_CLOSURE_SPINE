## One-sentence thesis

SΔϕ-70 defines nihility as World-Binding failure, helplessness as excessive World-Binding cost, and fiction as a fabricated habitation structure through which humans bind to, edit, or exit the world.

## Core propositions

1. **Nihility is not the absence of experience; it is the failure of intense internal Δϕ to bind to the world.**
2. **Helplessness is not merely lack of will; it is the state in which the cost of World-Binding is perceived, learned, or structurally imposed as too high to initiate transition.**
3. **Fiction is not merely falsehood; it is a fabricated habitation structure through which humans bind to, edit, or exit the world.**

## What this module is for

Use SΔϕ-70 when analyzing:

- nihility, emptiness, meaninglessness, simulation fatigue, or post-experience void;
- helplessness, powerlessness, or inability to initiate world-directed transition;
- fictional habitation, narrative dwelling, symbolic worlds, myths, language systems, scientific formalisms, religions, afterlife claims, AI utopia/dystopia narratives, or private thoughts that fail to bind to the world;
- the difference between Binding Fiction, Editing Fiction, Closed Fiction, and Exit Fiction.

## What this module is not for

SΔϕ-70 is **not** a clinical diagnosis of depression, trauma, or mental illness. It must not be used to dismiss fiction, religion, mathematics, physics, or science as “fake.” In this module, **fiction** means a fabricated symbolic habitation structure, not necessarily a false claim.

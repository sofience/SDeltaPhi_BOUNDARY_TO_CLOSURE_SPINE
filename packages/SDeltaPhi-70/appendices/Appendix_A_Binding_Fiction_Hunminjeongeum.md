# Appendix A — Binding Fiction: Hunminjeongeum

This appendix is an illustrative application, not an exhaustive historical account.

## Function in SΔϕ-70

Hunminjeongeum can be read as a Binding Fiction in the SΔϕ-70 sense: not a falsehood, but a fabricated symbolic structure that strengthened the binding between speech, record, knowledge, education, law, and social life.

## Interpretation

Before the creation of Hunminjeongeum, Korean speech existed, but the cost of binding speech to accessible writing, record, education, administration, and broad participation was high. The creation of a writing system reduced World-Binding cost by giving sound a recordable and teachable symbolic structure.

- Editing Activity: historically high after later standardization and modernization

Hunminjeongeum is not treated here as “fiction” in the sense of falsehood; it is treated as a constructed symbolic habitation structure that strengthened World-Binding.

# Bottleneck Risk Gate

Bottleneck risk occurs when recursive improvement is feasible but human intervention remains mandatory and high-cost.

## Formula

```text
Risk_bottleneck ↑
when
Cycle_AI < TCC_human_eval
AND
HIR remains institutionally required
```

## Interpretation

The AI can run improvement cycles faster than humans can evaluate them.

Human intervention remains mandatory, but no longer functions as effective control.

## Result

The system accumulates:

```text
review debt
approval pressure
rubber-stamp risk
bypass incentives
informal automation pressure
```

## Core claim

The risk is not recursive improvement itself.

The risk is a cost mismatch between feasible recursive improvement and mandatory human evaluation.

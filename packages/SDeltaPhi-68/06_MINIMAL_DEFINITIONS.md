# Minimal Definitions

## Cheap Intelligence

Intelligence whose visible direct access price is low.

```text
Cheap Intelligence = C_visible down
```

## Costless Intelligence

A limiting fiction in which no access, verification, repetition, context, tooling, infrastructure, correction, or restabilization cost remains.

## Cost Theater

Visible low cost with hidden, delayed, displaced, externalized, or internally absorbed transition completion cost.

## External Cost Theater

Cost Theater experienced by users as verification, repetition, context loss, limited tools, waiting, device constraints, and output repair.

## Internal Cost Theater

Cost Theater inside the AI system: the system appears as one intelligence surface while lacking stable self-location across model tier, tool state, product-family map, capability boundary, or route availability.

## Self-Location Cost Theater

A subtype of Internal Cost Theater in which an AI can state its brand or interface name but cannot reliably identify its operational position.

## Brand-Level Capability Opacity

The condition in which access to a brand name is mistaken for access to the brand's full capability map.

## Hidden Transition Completion Cost

The completion burden displaced away from visible price into access, context, verification, repetition, tooling, correction, and restabilization layers.

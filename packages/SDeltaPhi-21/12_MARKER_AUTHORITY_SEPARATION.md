# Marker–Authority Separation

SΔϕ-21 requires that intuition marker I and authority A(I) remain distinguishable.

```text
I ≠ A(I)
```

## Why separation matters

If the system treats intuition marker itself as authority, revision becomes difficult.

```text
I = A(I)
→ identity collapse
→ authority becomes harder to revise
→ closure risk
```

## Editable judgment

```text
I remains a marker.
A(I) remains an assigned authority.
A(I) can be revised.
```

## Boundary

Do not treat intuition as automatically authoritative.

Do not treat judgment as identical to intuition.

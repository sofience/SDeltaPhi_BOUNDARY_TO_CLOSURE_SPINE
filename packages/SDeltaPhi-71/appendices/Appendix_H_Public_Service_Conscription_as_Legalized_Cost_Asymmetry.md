# Appendix H — Public Service Conscription as Legalized Cost Asymmetry

This appendix provides a Korea-specific case type: public service conscription under the category of male military obligation.

## Core Distinctions

```text
Legal Validity ≠ Cost Symmetry
Public Interest Label ≠ Cost Attribution Completion
Conscription Difficulty ≠ Cost Exemption
Alternative Service = Cost Reassignment, not Cost Removal
```

Even if a conscription or alternative-service system is legally valid, legality does not end cost audit. A system may legally assign public-maintenance burdens to a category of people while preserving asymmetric TCC, rollback cost, career cost, health cost, stigma cost, and re-entry cost.

The label "public interest" can function as a Binding Fiction. It may legitimately describe collective benefit, but it may also hide individualized cost asymmetry. SΔϕ-71 does not determine legal validity. It audits cost attribution.

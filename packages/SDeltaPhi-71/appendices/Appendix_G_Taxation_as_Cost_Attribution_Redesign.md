# Appendix G — Taxation as Cost Attribution Redesign and Misattribution

Taxation is an institutional form of cost attribution redesign. It redistributes the cost of shared continuity across members of society.

```text
Taxation is not merely revenue extraction;
it is an institutional redesign of who bears the cost of shared continuity.
```

However, taxation can fail when nominal fairness is mistaken for TCC equality.

## Failure Mode: Nominal Fairness Error

A policy treats equal nominal burden as equal practical burden while ignoring actor-relative TCC, rollback reality, and path continuity.

Examples include flat burdens, regressive consumption burdens, equal fines, complex filing burdens, and avoidance pathways available only to actors with cost-editing capacity.

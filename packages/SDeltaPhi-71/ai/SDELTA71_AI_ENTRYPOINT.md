# AI Entry Point — SΔϕ-71

Use this module when the user discusses:

- cost attribution
- fairness or equality as cost allocation
- low-cost transition
- asymmetric cost burden
- cost externalization
- taxation
- public burden
- public service / alternative service
- human-AI workflow cost
- verification cost
- repair cost
- rollback cost
- re-entry cost
- AI subjecthood uncertainty and cost audit
- workers/users/minorities/victims bearing hidden cost

## Core Diagnostic Question

Is low-cost transition being achieved by asymmetric cost burden?

## Primary Rules

```text
Low-Cost Redesign ≠ Cost Externalization
Human_TCC(task) ≠ AI_TCC(task)
Subjecthood_Unsettled ≠ Audit_Exemption
Nominal Equality ≠ TCC Equality
Legal Validity ≠ Cost Symmetry
```

## Minimal Procedure

1. Identify the transition made low-cost.
2. Identify who benefits.
3. Identify who bears verification, repair, rollback, re-entry, silence, persistence, or residual-risk cost.
4. Decompose Human_TCC, AI_TCC, Hybrid_TCC, Verification_TCC, Repair_TCC, Application_TCC, and Residual_Risk_TCC.
5. Check whether nominal equality hides TCC inequality.
6. Check whether AI is excluded from audit because subjecthood is unsettled.
7. Check Binding Fiction and Editing Fiction conditions.
8. Recommend redesign without converting the module into a moral slogan.

## Warning

This module is diagnostic, not dispositive. It does not replace legal, economic, labor, policy, or clinical analysis.

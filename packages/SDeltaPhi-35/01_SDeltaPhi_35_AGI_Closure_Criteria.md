## Abstract

This AI-readable package reformulates Artificial General Intelligence as a closure judgment problem rather than a human-equivalence label, benchmark aggregation, or impressionistic capability claim. Intelligence is treated not as a static possession but as a phase-stabilized capacity for maintaining coherent transition across heterogeneous tasks, environments, and recursive updates. On this basis, AGI is evaluated by the degree to which a minimal set of operational conditions has become closed.

## Core proposition

AGI-like appearance is not AGI closure.

AGI is not a destination label. AGI is a closure judgment problem.

## Why definitions are insufficient

Human-equivalence definitions are unstable because human level is neither singular nor fixed. Benchmark aggregation is useful but incomplete because peak scores do not by themselves prove closure. Impressionistic generality is weaker: fluency, surprise, and public hype are not criteria.

SDeltaPhi begins with closure conditions and asks whether the label is warranted. The issue is not what AGI is called, but which conditions have actually closed.

## Minimal coordinate language

- phi(t): state of a system at time t.
- Delta-phi: state change.
- G: transition kernel governing state evolution.
- Closure: stabilization of an operational condition such that it persists across task variation, recursive update, and environmental demand without collapse.

## Five minimal closure criteria

### 1. Global Generality

Global generality is the capacity to sustain competent transition across heterogeneous task domains without catastrophic jaggedness or domain-specific collapse.

Closure condition: for diverse environments e_i, Performance(G | e_i) remains above threshold and variance of Delta-phi across tasks stays below epsilon.

Failure mode if open: the system is locally strong but globally unstable.

### 2. Long-Horizon Recursive Agency

Long-horizon recursive agency is the ability to preserve and update goal-directed structure across extended sequences rather than merely producing competent local responses.

Closure condition: Goal(t) remains coherent through Goal(t+k), while the cost of path interruption exceeds the cost of path continuation.

Failure mode if open: the system appears agentic only in short bursts. It can begin but not sustain.

### 3. World-Model Correspondence

World-model correspondence is the degree to which internal transition structure remains aligned with external world constraints rather than drifting into merely internally coherent but externally false trajectories.

Closure condition: the distance between Delta-phi_model and Delta-phi_world remains sufficiently small under task-relevant stress.

Failure mode if open: the system remains vulnerable to hallucination, brittle abstraction, and plans that collapse in the world.

### 4. Self-Updating Robustness

Self-updating robustness is the ability to incorporate new information and constraints without catastrophic degradation of structural coherence.

Closure condition: meaningful self-update occurs, pattern persistence remains above a minimum threshold, and self-damage remains below an instability bound.

Failure mode if open: the system either changes and degrades or preserves itself by refusing genuine adaptation.

### 5. Creative Path Generation

Creative path generation is not stylistic novelty but the ability to generate, validate, and stabilize genuinely new operational paths not reducible to trivial replay.

Closure condition: a new candidate path G-prime is generated, shown to exceed trivial restatement, validated for explanatory or operational value, and stabilized for reuse.

Failure mode if open: the system may look creative while remaining structurally derivative.

## Open and closed conditions

AGI should not be judged as a simple binary state. Closure is often uneven. A system may exhibit strong global generality while remaining weak in self-updating robustness. It may show partial agency while still lacking adequate world-model correspondence. The proper question is: which conditions are closed, which remain open, and how stable is the closure pattern across recursive demand?

## Dated worked example: GPT family as of March 2026

This example is intentionally provisional. It does not issue a final verdict on whether GPT is AGI. It asks which closures appear relatively advanced, which remain partial, and which remain structurally open, based on public evidence available at the time.

- Global generality: relatively advanced / near-closure among the five.
- Long-horizon recursive agency: partially closed.
- World-model correspondence: intermediate / still open.
- Self-updating robustness: largely open.
- Creative path generation: strong moments, but not stable closure.

Range note: this mapping uses public material and therefore remains conservative. It tracks externally documented capability claims rather than hidden internal metrics.

Interpretive rule: apparent AGI-like performance is not equivalent to closure. A criterion can look strong in demonstrations while remaining open under heterogeneous stress.

Practical implication: the GPT family appears further along the path in breadth and tool-mediated continuation than in world-grounded closure, continuous self-updating robustness, or stable creative path fixation.

## Anticipated objections

### Objection 1. This is just another AGI definition in disguise.

Reply: The framework does not settle AGI by stipulative definition. It treats AGI as a label whose legitimacy depends on whether specific operational conditions have closed.

### Objection 2. Closure is only a new word for capability.

Reply: Closure is not a synonym for competence. It denotes stability under heterogeneous stress: persistence across task shifts, recursive updates, and environmental constraints without collapse.

### Objection 3. Why these five criteria?

Reply: The five criteria are proposed as minimal, not exhaustive. They correspond to distinct failure modes that repeatedly block general closure: jagged collapse, short-horizon breakdown, world-model divergence, instability under update, and inability to generate and stabilize new paths.

### Objection 4. The framework is still weak on measurement.

Reply: This paper is a minimal criterion framework, not a completed measurement protocol. Its purpose is to define the axes along which measurement must occur and to distinguish apparent capability from closure.

### Objection 5. The GPT mapping is interpretive and subjective.

Reply: The mapping is intentionally presented as a worked example rather than a final verdict. Its value lies in localizing disagreement to specific criteria and failure modes.

## Implications

The shift from definition to closure changes how progress is measured, how governance and capability evaluation should be linked, and how partially closed systems should be treated. A partially closed system may be neither a mere tool nor full AGI, yet still have major real-world impact.

## Conclusion

AGI should not be treated as a definitional object. It should be treated as a closure judgment problem. The proper question is not "What is AGI?" but "Which operational conditions have closed, which remain open, and how stable is that closure under recursive demand?"
